# Shopping-experience
An exploratory data analysis (EDA) project examining customer shopping behavior across demographics, product categories, payment preferences, and purchase patterns. The dataset was cleaned, transformed, and analyzed in Excel to surface actionable insights on customer spending and retention.

---

## Objectives

- Clean and prepare raw shopping data
- Explore customer purchasing behaviour
- Identify shopping trends
- Analyse customer satisfaction
- Build an interactive dashboard
- Generate actionable business insights

---
## Key Findings
-	Revenue by category: Clothing leads total revenue ($104,264), followed by Accessories ($74,200), Footwear ($36,093), and Outerwear ($18,524) — out of $233,081 total.
- Customer mix: New customers (2,272) outnumber returning customers (1,628), with Clothing and Accessories the top categories for both groups.
-	Gender split: 68% of purchases come from male customers vs. 32% from female customers, with Clothing the top category for both.
-	Payment preferences: Fairly even spread across PayPal, Credit Card, Cash, Debit Card, Venmo, and Bank Transfer (each ~600–680 transactions).
-	Shipping type revenue: Revenue is evenly distributed across all six shipping types (Free Shipping, Express, Store Pickup, Standard, 2-Day, Next Day Air), each contributing roughly $38K–$41K.
-	Seasonality: Purchases are fairly stable year-round, with Fall ($60,018) slightly ahead of Spring, Winter, and Summer.
-	Most popular color: Olive (177 purchases), followed closely by Yellow, Silver, and Teal.
